My Work at Cummins
IT Systems Analyst Placement Student
Project Etoile
In the fourth month of my placement, I joined “Project Etoile,” supporting Cummins’ expansion into France by integrating the Lyon and Nantes sites into Cummins’ IT infrastructure and policies. As part of the IT integration team, my role focused on deploying new Cummins devices to users at the Lyon site and transferring both professional and personal data from their old devices. I travelled to Lyon with two experienced IT team members to complete this task. My responsibilities included setting up 50 users with new laptops tailored to their software needs, providing bilingual training resources, ensuring daily updates to the S and V drives, and serving as a support contact for French users. Key challenges included managing time around user schedules and overcoming the language barrier. To address this, I created individual setup schedules and developed French-English support materials, which improved efficiency and user satisfaction. A major concern was the secure transfer of personal data, which could contain sensitive information. To mitigate risks, I studied the data transfer scripts in detail, monitored outputs, and tracked progress closely. During the final data push, the script completed in under a minute instead of the expected three hours, with no clear error. Drawing on my earlier script study, I identified that the disk reference letter had defaulted incorrectly. After correcting the script, the data transfer completed successfully. This experience underscored the importance of understanding technical processes, especially in coding and troubleshooting, and highlighted the value of preparation, adaptability, and clear communication in cross-cultural IT projects.
Onboarding Buddy
At the end of my placement at Cummins, I was tasked with creating resources and delivering a week of training for my successor. Alongside my colleague Rida, we developed an AI agent designed to support new starters by hosting company information, HR tutorials, and task-specific guidance. We collaborated with the innovation team leading the AI initiative, using their template and insights to shape our approach. To validate the need, we conducted surveys and interviews with placement students and consulted the onboarding team to explore broader applications for new employees. I learned the value of strong documentation and data-driven pitching, which significantly strengthened our proposal. During our research, we discovered a similar project underway in the engineering department. We redirected our pitch to the lead engineer, resulting in a successful collaboration. This led to our involvement in pilot testing AI vendors like Glean and ChatGPT. Comparing their performance for our use case was insightful, especially in identifying hallucination patterns and refining agent tone and instructions. We later joined educational sessions with the innovation team, showcasing our agent and contributing to broader testing. I’m proud of the impact our “Onboarding Buddy” will have and look forward to seeing how it evolves to support future new starters.

Estimated overall value

  • ■ Supplying an onboarding agent to the UK intern workforce can deliver over $225k in productivity savings per year
  • ■ An onboarding agent for all new Cummins employees could deliver productivity savings of between $7.5M and $14M annually, dependent on number of new hires in a given year
  • ■ New value capture from the hours saved is conservatively estimated to be between $11M-$23M a year
  • ■ Time saved for senior employees (moving new starters to self service) is $340k/yr
Power Apps
One of my main responsibilities this past year was to create, improve and maintain applications used within different areas of the business. The applications can be seen in Movex, Incremental discount, Strategic Stock, MRC requests etc. During the first month I created a Movex Intake Request application where users can submit issues and check their progress, while the IT team work on them. This application provided a structure for the weekly sprint planning meetings, saving unproductive time, and ensured the correct people were being notified for scheduled tasks. Another notable piece of work was streamlining the IDR application which previously required users to pre download forms to be submitted within the application, which caused time wasting of reloading pages and often the wrong types of form being used. Instead, I added a download feature to the application which opened the correct form in a new tab based on the type of request selected. During my time creating and maintaining applications I was able to apply my knowledge of application design learnt in my human computer interaction module at university. I found a lot of existing applications within Cummins had great functionality, but users often found them too complicated to use so I aimed to simplify them, applying the principles form the course. I see an opportunity in the future to create a management system to host all the company’s existing applications as I believe a lot of copies of application are made as people are unaware they exist. It would also important to check that all application currently in use are compatible with SAP or can be modified to be compatible, otherwise we risk facing downtime. Estimated overall value ■ Structure provided to sprint planning meetings saving 15 minutes per week of unproductive time ■ Time saved of users navigating download forms and waiting for a response estimated 10 minutes of delay per request put in ■ Estimated time saved of creating supplicate applications at a minimum of 30 minutes per application
Power Automate
The past year I have worked greatly with Power Automate to create flows that automate manual tasks, as well as modifying flows when there is an issue. I was able to use my knowledge in coding and flow diagrams to build these processes and apply them to different areas of the business. The first manual action I replaced was the manual file uploading for reports, which previously required an employee to download and upload email attachments to the correct area inside a SharePoint site. I saw this as a waste of time and created a flow to trigger when the email was received and automatically save the attachment to the correct location with the correct name. This ensured that all files followed the naming convention set and saved an estimated 30 minutes of work per week. The principle of this flow was then replicated in the uploading of scanner files in Poland which saved an estimated 45 minutes of work per week. I got lots of opportunities to apply my trouble shooting skills to existing flows like the Movex Access Request Flow which often was getting stuck. I found this was because it was waiting for the wrong type of approval which was also the case for the WWID based access flow. While working on a fix for the strategic stock flow, I noticed that a lot of requests remain unattended and do not progress in the flow which causes an issue as if it runs longer than 30 days the request stops. I implemented a reminder mechanism to send notifications out to the employees were the request was pending to that occurred from 14 days of sitting idle, which reduced the amount of stopped requests by half. Something that I have pushed for within Power Automate is creating documentation for any processes created. I found it much more difficult to improve a flow where I did not know the function or why actions were placed there, but when I started to create documentation for them revisiting was a lot easier. I also found that when given detailed documentation the initial requesters of the flow were able to make changes themselves. Estimated value ■ Automated uploads of email documents and printer scans estimated at 60 minutes per week ■ Time saved from reuploading stuck and failed requests by issuing reminders average 5 minutes per request, 10 requests every 30 days ■ Documentation created reduces the learning time of a process by 20 minutes
Swedish Student Project
At the beginning of January myself and fellow placement student Rida were given the opportunity to collaborate with some Swedish student’s university project. We were given guidance by Andrew and Ram about the nature of the problem statement we presented the students with but also had the independence to generate our own data and lead the supporting sessions. The problem statement created outlined that the existing ticketing system we had in place was inefficient and was generating an excessive work load. To improve the system, we wanted to explore the possibility of using an AI driven solution and compare architectures to see what would be the best fit. To begin with myself and Rida generated some generic ticket data basing it off common user requests but not using any exact duplicates to protect data privacy. The students then analyzed the data to see if an AI solution would be beneficial, it was important to determine if it would make a difference based on what type most of the tickets were. Rida and I supported the process with weekly meetings to answer any questions and offer any ideas we had to help categorize the ticket types, which resulted in a conclusion that 70% could be handled by AI. For the next 3 months we continued these meetings to discuss different architectures and their pros and cons. We were able to connect with others at Cummins to get diagrams of how the existing ticketing system works which helped to explain to the girls. We previewed their presentations and reports before they were submitted, offered advice on any edits and set up some time for them to present to our IT team and the AME team. I thoroughly enjoyed the process of working with these girls as they were similarly aged and like minded to both Rida and me. It was a great opportunity to test our knowledge of our systems by explaining it to someone else and I learnt a lot about different AI solutions through reading their paper. We received feedback after they had submitted, and I was delighted to hear they enjoyed working with us both and that one of the girls had got herself an internship and gave credit to this project. It was very rewarding to know we had helped someone and I will look for opportunities to do it again.
SharePoint Sites
A large majority of the applications and flows I have worked on have been linked to SharePoint sites, either by lists or file storage. This meant that I got quite experienced in editing sites for each of their purposes and was able to start creating new sites when new opportunities arose. A lot of the sites I have edited were done to become more accessible for users as they were struggling to find files they needed access to. I have implemented folder structure within the strategic stock site and the Incremental discount request site which reduced user questions and support. I also moved a lot of PowerApps to be hosted within SharePoint sites to ensure that data was being entered correctly into forms. Particularly in the Movex access site, users were entering information directly into the list which did not perform validation, but by moving the app to the site they were forced to enter information into the application which was validated first before it got entered into the list and started a flow. During Project Etoile I connected with the HR department in Lyon who expressed the need to have a site to communicate announcements and changes to the office instead of detailing everything in emails. I requested the creation of this site and offered a tutorial to the HR team on how to edit the site to get it how they want it to be. At the beginning of the year, I was in discussion of creating a new document management system with a longer retention period for some project data. After some vendor meetings we concluded to keep using SharePoint but to extend the period on the site, which required a new site to be created. As this site layout was to be a copy of a previous one I managed to create an automated flow to copy each page across and then I manually changed some links to ensure they pointed to the correct site location. Previously this task would be estimated to take a week but with my process it only took a day, which meant we were ready to use it earlier. This same site is used to share information with external users, so another task is to maintain the access levels granted to different groups.
